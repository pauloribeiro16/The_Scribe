According to Article 17 of the GDPR, individuals have the right to request the deletion of their personal data, including the erasure of data that is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.  This right is granted to individuals, regardless of their legal capacity, and it’s a crucial component of the GDPR’s broader principle of data minimization and purpose limitation.

Here's a breakdown based on the relevant articles and recital:

**The Core of the Right:**

Article 17, Section 3 of the GDPR provides the individual with the right to request the deletion of their personal data. This right is not unlimited; it’s balanced against the legitimate interests of the data controller(s). However, the fundamental principle is that individuals should have control over their data.

**Conditions for Data Erasure:**

The GDPR outlines specific conditions for this right to be exercised.  These conditions are designed to ensure data is handled responsibly and securely. Key conditions include:

*   **Data is no longer necessary:** This is the primary basis for erasure. If the data is no longer needed for the original purposes for which it was collected, it should be erased.
*   **Data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected:** If the data is retained for a purpose for which it is no longer needed, deletion is permissible.
*   **Data is insufficient:**  If the data is insufficient for the purposes for which it was collected, it should be erased.
*   **Data is inaccurate:** If the data is inaccurate, it should be erased.
*   **Data has been erased:** If the data has been erased, it should be erased.

**Recitals Supporting the Right:**

The GDPR cites several key articles and recitals that support this right. Specifically:

*   **Article 6(1)(e):**  This article establishes the right to erasure as a fundamental right of individuals. It provides the basis for understanding the right to erasure, referring to the requirement for data minimization and purpose limitation.
*   **Recital 3:** This recital emphasizes the importance of the right to erasure, highlighting the balance between individual rights and the legitimate interests of data controllers. It further emphasizes that data should be erased only when it is no longer necessary.
*   **Recital 4:** This recital explains that data controllers have a duty to implement appropriate measures to ensure that data is managed securely and that individuals can exercise their rights.

**In essence, Article 17 allows individuals to request the deletion of their data if it’s no longer relevant or necessary for the purposes for which it was collected. The conditions are carefully crafted to ensure this right is exercised responsibly.**
